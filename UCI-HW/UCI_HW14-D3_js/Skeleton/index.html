US Health Analysis


FINDINGS

Health Level

Food Stamps

There is a strong negative correlation (-0.69) between states' populations' dependency on food stamps (ON and OFF Food Stamps) and health level. A state that is more heavily dependent on entitlements as seen with Puerto Rico tend to have populations in poorer health. The inverse is also true, populations less dependent on stamps such as New Hampshire with 92.1% not reliant on food stamps have a much healthier population with 60% in positive health.

Education

There is a strong positive correlation (0.83) between a populations' education (high school diplomas and greater) and health level. A largely educated populace as seen with Minnessota has 92.6% with high school diplomas and greater has a much higher positive health level with 57.7%. Similarly, the inverse is true, populations found largely without high school diplomas as in Puerto Rico (26.1%) -- highest of any state -- have populations with the least in positive health (31%).

Medical Checkup Within Past 1 Year

Food Stamps

There is a weak negative correlation (-0.42) between states' populations' dependency on food stamps (ON & OFF Food Stamps) and past 1 year medical checkup. A state that is more heavily dependent on entitlements as seen with Puerto Rico, 38.7% on stamps, have a higher proportion of past 1 year checkups, 76.4%. The inverse is also true, populations less dependent on stamps such as Utah with 8.1% not reliant on food stamps have a much lower checkup rate 58%.

Education

There is a weak positive correlation (0.35) between a populations' education (high school diplomas and greater) and past 1 year medical checkup. A largely educated populace as seen with Utah has 91.4% with high school diplomas and greater has one of the lowest past 1 year medical check up rate of 58%. In contrast, populations with a lower education level as in Puerto Rico (73.9%) -- lowest of any state -- have populations with higher medical checkup rates, 76.4%.
